    This Week's Picks

    AFC Championship

  • Denver Broncos vs. New England Patriots

    • Brady vs. Manning one last time. It was destined to be this way, wasn't it? What a delight for sports fans everywhere. On one side, we have the healthy-just-in-time Patriots whose offense finally seems to be clicking again. On the other, we have the Broncos who somehow have stumbled their way into hosting this conference championship with the skeleton of Peyton Manning lofting ducks into the secondary. The homefield advantage will make it close, but I pick Bill and Tom to lead New England to a reason-defying 7th Super Bowl in the last 15 years (9th in franchise history).



    NFC Championship

  • Carolina Panthers vs. Arizona Cardinals

    • Both of these teams had to frantically defend late leads in the divisional round and barely escaped despite seeming to have the game firmly in hand. No matter now as Cam Newton leads his merry bunch of "dabbers" into battle against Carson Palmer and Larry Fitzgerald, the ageless wonder who saved the Cardinals from what might have been the most painful defeat in NFL playoff history-- giving up two Hail Mary's to the Packers in the span of one minute. I expect Carolina's crowd to lift Newton and co. to victory, but it won't be easy against a very impressive Arizona secondary. Look for another close game that comes down to the wire.

    NFC Divisional
    • Carolina Panthers vs. Seattle Seahawks Success

      • What a game this will be! Newton's 15-1 Panthers taking on Wilson's lucky-to-still-be-here Seahawks. I expect we will finally see a game with some scoring and this should be the most entertaining of the weekend. Ultimately though, Carolina has too much offensive firepower. The Panthers will host the NFC Championship.

    • Arizona Cardinals vs. Green Bay Packers Success

      • The Packers looked great in the Wild Card round, but I suspect a large portion of that had to do with their opponent. It will be a different ballgame in Arizona, where Carson Palmer and the Cardinals await a Packers team that has looked unusually vulnerable this season. I expect the Cards to roll, setting up a 1-2, 1-2 Final Four in the AFC and NFC.
